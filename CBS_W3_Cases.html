H8 · Insurance Chatbot

Complexity 2/5

The Insurance Chatbot That Retrieved the Wrong Truth

The scenario

A Scandinavian insurance company replaces static policy documentation with a GenAI chatbot grounded via RAG in its actual policy documents. The system is praised internally: hallucinations are near-zero because answers come from real documents. What the team did not build: a versioning system for the RAG index. Policy documents are updated quarterly. The RAG index is refreshed annually.

The organisation

Major Nordic insurer. 2.3 million customers. GenAI chatbot live across web, app, and call centre.

The gap

A flood coverage exclusion was added in Q3 2026. The chatbot continues to confirm coverage from the Q2 document.

The Central Bank That Automated Monetary Policy

The scenario

A Eurozone central bank deploys an agentic AI system to recommend interest rate decisions in real time. The system processes LLM-analysed news, market data, and economic indicators, trained on 30 years of monetary policy decisions. In its first live quarter, it recommends four consecutive rate cuts. H2 · Hospital Triage

Complexity 3/5

The Hospital That Outsourced Triage to an Agent

The scenario

A Danish hospital deploys an agentic AI triage system. It reads intake forms, retrieves patient history from the EHR, prioritises patients, and autonomously allocates them to treatment queues. A patient with atypical symptom presentation — consistent with both a minor condition and a rare cardiac event — is de-prioritised. The rare condition was underrepresented in training data. The agentic loop had no re-evaluation trigger once a patient was classified.

The organisation

Regional Danish hospital. 180,000 outpatient contacts per year. Triage system deployed across all emergency intake.

The failure point

The plan-act-observe loop acted on intake data and never re-observed. No mechanism for a classified patient to re-enter priority assessment.
